About

I'm Belinda. I'm currently in the first year of a PhD in the School of Psychology at the University of New South Wales (UNSW, Sydney). I'm currently look at how people jump to conclusions - and how they jump differently after seeing information again and again (hello insidious news cycle...)

I was born and raised in Perth, where I completed my Bachelor of Science (Honours) at the University of Western Australia. In my Honours project, I looked at how individuals make decisions about climate change. I found that individuals used a lot of faulty assumptions, but a lot of these assumptions went away when individuals talked to other individuals in groups! This taught me that we mustn't lose hope in humans. Also, talk to other humans.

When I'm not thinking about how people think, I like to run away to places with open skies and unreliable phone service. When I'm not being a privileged faux-hippie, I try to hear the music, eat the food, and see the films that come as perks to living in a big city.

Do get in touch if you want to hear more about my research.
